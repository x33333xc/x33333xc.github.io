Abstract & Method

Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) have recently attracted significant attention due to their advanced capability in photorealistic view synthesis. However, NeRF-based approaches represent scene as a collection of independently sampled points and construct each sample point as a radiance field with independent color and volume density attributes, lacking the modeling capacity for spatial dependencies. Consequently, the spatial relationships within the scene are not fully exploited, leading to a view synthesis quality that falls short of achieving a photorealistic level. In this paper, we propose a specialized neural radiance field called MixNeRF, specifically designed to address the limitations of NeRF-based methods, significantly enhancing their performance. The core idea of MixNeRF is to perform local fusion using dynamic kernels. First, we fuse radiance values for the same ray and different rays in the radiance field space. Subsequently, we perform local fusion in the color domain for the synthesized images, introducing a novel approach to ensure view consistency. Through extensive qualitative and quantitative analysis via experiments, our method demonstrates substantial performance improvements on both synthetic and real-world datasets compared to NeRF-based approaches.
